My Hometown - Surabaya

About Surabaya

Surabaya, the capital of East Java province, stands as one of Indonesia's largest and most dynamic cities. Known as "The City of Heroes" (Kota Pahlawan), Surabaya holds a crucial place in the history of Indonesia's struggle for independence. It's not just a major hub for trade and business in the eastern part of the country, but also a repository of rich history, unique culture, and delicious cuisine.

Climate and People

Surabaya has a typical tropical climate with two distinct seasons: a warm and sunny dry season from April to October, and a rainy season from November to March. Though often hot, the sea breeze from the Madura Strait offers some relief. The people of Surabaya, affectionately called "Arek Suroboyo," are known for their warmth, strong fighting spirit, and candidness. They share a strong sense of community and deeply value brotherhood.

Economy and Education

As a metropolitan city, Surabaya is a hub for trade, industry, and education in eastern Indonesia. The Port of Tanjung Perak is one of the nation's busiest, facilitating both domestic and international trade. The city is also home to numerous major corporations and industries, from manufacturing to the service sector. In terms of education, Surabaya boasts many top-tier universities that attract students from all over Indonesia, making it a multicultural and innovative city.
